Sonic: Textures

Key Roles: 3D Modelling, Texture Art, UI and VFX

Summary:

Collaborated with Gamemode One to bring Sonic the Hedgehog's iconic world into Minecraft, again! Created high-quality 3D models, textures, particles and developed user interface graphics for Minecraft‚Äôs classic heads up display.

Key Contributions:

Above is a set of 3 critters I modelled and textured. While the frog takes direct inspiration from a Sonic character named Froggy and the Rabbit takes direct inspiration from the a Sonic critter called Pocky. The ram, is an original critter created in the style of other critters specifically for Minecraft.

A problem that I really enjoyed on this project is the Minecraft Villagers. I wanted them to be recognizable as classic Minecraft villagers, but have a sense of wonder to them. I settled on anthropomorphic animals, as you see in the Sonic franchise. I was the sole owner of this feature, creating the models, textures, implementation, etc.

Each villager is assigned an anthropomorphic model on spawn, they then roll a random number between 1 and 6 which assigns them their preset random base colour. Their last layer is their profession, depending on which profession they are assigned, they will wear 1 of 15 outfits. This results in the possibility of 47,829,690,000,000 different villagers.

I also re-designed the entire Minecraft HUD inspired by various elements of Sonic the Hedgehog‚Äôs history. Such as rings representing health, chili dogs representing hunger or the hot bar containing the dirt from Green Hill Zone.

Avatar: Legends

Key Roles: Technical Art, Game design and VFX

Summary:

Collaborated with Gamemode One to bring the Avatar the Last Airbender to the Minecraft Marketplace as well as continued development on the free ‚ÄúSky Bison Update‚Äù that allowed the player to raise and ride their own Sky Bison.

Key Contributions:

Had ownership over each elements visual style. The product contains each element showcased in the show, Air, Earth, Fire, Water. The player could utilize 4 bending techniques per elemental domain. Thus I created the visual effects for 16 different elemental techniques.

Created a system that allowed us to mimic outline shaders inside Minecraft. At the moment of release, Minecraft‚Äôs engine does not allow creators to utilize custom shaders. Instead I used the tools we had to create a system that gave entities black outlines to mimic the black line art that is seen in the shows and comics.

The system was also use to communicate to the player, entities that the player could interact with. With a little bit of trigonometry I was able to create a system that outlines the entity in white if the player is looking at it and at a certain distance. Similar to how the mirror effect was achieved in another project I worked on, Spellrune.

About

I have always been fascinated by the intersection of creativity and technology. From the age of 12, I immersed myself in game development learning Gamemaker 6, honing my skills and pushing the boundaries of what I could create.

This passion led to opportunities to collaborate with some of the biggest names in the entertainment industry, including Bandai Namco, SEGA, DreamWorks, and Nickelodeon. I have shipped 12 pieces of content through the Minecraft Marketplace. I have had the unique opportunity to work with iconic franchises like Pac-Man, Sonic the Hedgehog, Avatar the Last Airbender, and How to Train Your Dragon

I have recently picked up product design. I run an online shop on Etsy called Makeshape where I create functional designs to be 3D printed. This venture has given me the opportunity to learn how to build trusting relationships between customers and my brand. It also gives me the chance to take my digital skills, such as 3D modeling and deliver a physical item in reality.

Beyond professional work, I find myself returning to game development in my spare time, utilizing Gamemaker Studio, some of my projects can be found on Itch.io, Google Play Store and the depths of my hard drive. Returning to this familiar space allows me to focus on aspects of game development that I currently do not have the opportunity of exploring professionally.
